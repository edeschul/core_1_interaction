Mother nature can be very powerful and unpredictable. I grew up in the Bay Area, so the only severe weather I dealt with were the occassional rainstorms and small earthquakes. However in other parts of the world, people deal with tsunamis, hurricanes, and floods.

What is a Tsunami?

A tsunami is a series of large ocean waves, caused by the disturbances between tectonic plates. Tsunamis are capable of reaching speeds at 500 to 800 kilometers per hour, which is about equivalent to the speed that commercial airplanes fly.

What is a Hurricane?

A hurricane is a tropical cyclone characterized by strong winds, rain, and thunderstorms. There are three categorizations of hurricanes; Typhoons, cyclones, and hurricanes. Each name is dependent on where they formed. Typhoons are formed in the Western Pacific Ocean, cyclones are formed in the Indian Ocean and South Pacific, and hurricanes are formed in the Atlantic Ocean and the eastern Pacific Ocean.

What causes Floods?

Floods are caused by an exccessive amount of rainfall, overflowment of rivers, storm surges, melting snow/ice, dam and levee breaks, and tsunamis (to name a few). Flash floods are quite dangerous because of how sudden they can occur. Floods might look like a giant swimming pool, but they can be very powerful. Just at 6 inches of fast-moving floodwater can knock an adult over.
